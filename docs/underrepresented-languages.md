# Underrepresented Languages

Modern multilingual AI should support the full diversity of the world's languages, not only those with abundant digital resources.

Many languages remain underrepresented in AI and NLP because of historical, political, educational, economic, or technological factors rather than linguistic importance.

Within the Multilingual Benchmark Explorer, underrepresented languages provide valuable opportunities to study multilingual AI engineering, tokenization, embeddings, retrieval, evaluation, translation, and cross-lingual transfer while helping build more inclusive language technologies.

---

# Why underrepresented languages matter

Many languages have limited digital resources available for training, evaluating, and deploying modern AI systems.

These limitations may include:

- limited digital corpora
- relatively few annotated datasets
- limited NLP tooling
- fewer pretrained models
- limited benchmark coverage
- limited speech resources
- limited educational technologies

Improving AI support for these languages contributes to more inclusive multilingual systems while advancing AI engineering research.

# Research themes

## Cross-lingual transfer

Questions include:

- Can higher-resource languages improve performance for related lower-resource languages?
- How much knowledge transfers within the same language family?
- Which multilingual models transfer knowledge most effectively?

---

## Language contact

Questions include:

- Can historical language contact improve multilingual AI?
- How do bilingual communities influence multilingual representations?
- Can borrowed vocabulary improve retrieval or translation?

See:

- `language-contact.md`

---

## Morphology

Research questions include:

- How efficiently do multilingual tokenizers represent morphologically rich languages?
- Which embedding models best preserve morphological information?
- How does morphology influence multilingual evaluation?

---

## Retrieval

Research questions include:

- Can multilingual retrieval improve access to educational resources?
- Which embedding models best support lower-resource languages?
- How should multilingual retrieval be evaluated?

---

## Translation

Research questions include:

# Ethical considerations

Responsible multilingual AI should prioritize collaboration with language communities.

Important considerations include:

- community involvement
- cultural preservation
- transparency
- accessibility
- responsible AI
- data ownership

Engineering success should be measured not only by benchmark performance but also by whether technology meaningfully supports language communities.
