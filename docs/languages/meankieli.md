# Meänkieli

## AI engineering profile

Meänkieli is a Finnic language spoken primarily in Sweden's Torne Valley and recognized as one of Sweden's official national minority languages.

Closely related to Finnish while shaped by centuries of contact with Swedish, Meänkieli provides an exceptional case study for multilingual AI because it combines language-family relationships, language contact, multilingual education, public-sector language technology, and national minority language support.

Within the Multilingual Benchmark Explorer, Meänkieli serves as the flagship language for investigating multilingual AI systems that support national minority languages.

---

## Why Meänkieli is an important benchmark

Meänkieli provides an outstanding benchmark because it combines:

- close relationship with Finnish
- centuries of contact with Swedish
- official national minority language status
- comparatively limited digital resources
- multilingual education
- multilingual public services

Rather than representing a limitation, these characteristics make Meänkieli an ideal language for understanding how multilingual AI can support minority language communities.

## Why this language belongs in the Multilingual Benchmark Explorer

Meänkieli serves as the repository's flagship national minority language.

Its close relationship with Finnish, centuries of contact with Swedish, and official recognition within Sweden make it an exceptional case study for understanding how multilingual AI systems can support bilingual communities, multilingual public services, educational technology, and national minority languages.

Rather than viewing Meänkieli solely as a lower-resource language, this repository treats it as an opportunity to investigate multilingual AI in real-world social, educational, and governmental settings.
