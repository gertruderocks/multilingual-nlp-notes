# Udmurt

## AI engineering profile

Udmurt is a Uralic language within the Permic branch and one of the most important lower-resource languages for investigating multilingual AI across the Uralic language family.

Although Udmurt has fewer digital resources than Finnish or Estonian, it provides valuable opportunities to study multilingual embeddings, tokenization, retrieval, and cross-lingual transfer within a distinct branch of the Uralic family.

Within the Multilingual Benchmark Explorer, Udmurt serves as the flagship representative of the Permic languages.

---

## Why Udmurt is an important benchmark

Udmurt provides an excellent benchmark because it combines:

- rich agglutinative morphology
- limited digital resources
- representation of the Permic branch
- opportunities for multilingual transfer
- valuable lower-resource AI challenges

This makes Udmurt particularly useful for evaluating how multilingual AI systems generalize across the diversity of the Uralic language family.

## Why this language belongs in the Multilingual Benchmark Explorer

Udmurt serves as the repository's flagship representative of the Permic branch of the Uralic language family.

Its combination of rich morphology, lower-resource status, Cyrillic writing system, and regional multilingualism makes it an important case study for understanding how multilingual AI systems generalize across different branches of the Uralic family while accounting for morphology, resource availability, and language contact.
