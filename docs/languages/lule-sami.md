# Lule Sámi

## AI engineering profile

Lule Sámi is an Indigenous Uralic language spoken primarily across northern Sweden and Norway.

Although comparatively limited in digital resources, Lule Sámi provides an exceptional case study for multilingual AI because it combines cross-border language communities, multilingual public services, Indigenous language technology, and multilingual information access.

Within the Multilingual Benchmark Explorer, Lule Sámi serves as the flagship case study for investigating multilingual AI systems that support cross-border Indigenous language communities.

---

## Why Lule Sámi is an important benchmark

Lule Sámi provides an outstanding benchmark because it combines:

- rich agglutinative morphology
- comparatively limited digital resources
- cross-border language use
- multilingual public services
- Indigenous language technology
- growing digital language resources

Rather than representing a limitation, these characteristics make Lule Sámi an ideal language for investigating multilingual AI systems operating across national boundaries.
