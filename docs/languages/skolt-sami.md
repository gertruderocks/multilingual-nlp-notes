# Skolt Sámi

## AI engineering profile

Skolt Sámi is an Indigenous Uralic language within the Sámi branch, spoken primarily in northeastern Finland, with smaller communities in Norway and Russia.

Although Skolt Sámi has comparatively limited digital resources, it provides an exceptional case study for multilingual AI because it allows researchers to investigate multilingual transfer, lower-resource language technology, Indigenous AI, and hierarchical transfer between closely related languages.

Within the Multilingual Benchmark Explorer, Skolt Sámi serves as a flagship case study for multilingual transfer among closely related Indigenous languages.

---

## Why Skolt Sámi is an important benchmark

Skolt Sámi provides an outstanding benchmark because it combines:

- rich agglutinative morphology
- comparatively limited digital resources
- close relationships with other Sámi languages
- multilingual communities
- cross-border language use
- opportunities for hierarchical multilingual transfer

Rather than representing a limitation, these characteristics make Skolt Sámi an ideal language for investigating how multilingual AI can support very small language communities.

## Why this language belongs in the Multilingual Benchmark Explorer

Skolt Sámi serves as the repository's flagship case study for hierarchical multilingual transfer among closely related Indigenous languages.

It provides a unique opportunity to investigate whether multilingual AI systems can leverage knowledge learned from higher-resource languages—including Finnish and North Sámi—to better support smaller Indigenous language communities.

This represents one of the repository's most interesting engineering questions: **Can multilingual AI scale support from larger languages to closely related lower-resource languages without requiring enormous amounts of new training data?**
