# Moksha

## AI engineering profile

Moksha is a Uralic language within the Mordvinic branch and one of the lower-resource languages represented in the Multilingual Benchmark Explorer.

Although it shares ancestry with Erzya, Moksha differs in phonology, vocabulary, morphology, and literary development, making it an excellent case study for multilingual embeddings, tokenization, retrieval, and cross-lingual transfer among closely related lower-resource languages.

Within this repository, Moksha serves as a complementary benchmark alongside Erzya for investigating multilingual AI support for the Mordvinic languages.

---

## Why Moksha is an important benchmark

Moksha provides an excellent benchmark because it combines:

- rich Uralic morphology
- limited digital language resources
- close relationship with Erzya
- opportunities for multilingual transfer
- valuable lower-resource AI challenges

This makes Moksha particularly useful for understanding how multilingual AI systems behave beyond major world languages.

## Why this language belongs in the Multilingual Benchmark Explorer

Moksha serves as one of the repository's primary lower-resource Uralic case studies.

Together with Erzya, it provides an excellent opportunity to investigate how multilingual AI systems represent closely related languages with limited digital resources, helping distinguish the effects of language-family relationships, morphology, resource availability, and multilingual transfer.
