# Turkish

## AI engineering profile

Turkish is one of the world's most important languages for studying multilingual AI.

As a Turkic language with highly productive agglutinative morphology, Turkish provides an outstanding benchmark for investigating tokenization, embeddings, retrieval, multilingual transfer, and language modeling.

Within the Multilingual Benchmark Explorer, Turkish serves as the primary non-Uralic agglutinative language, making it an ideal comparison language for Finnish, Estonian, Hungarian, and Azerbaijani.

---

## Why Turkish is an important benchmark

Turkish provides an excellent benchmark because it combines:

- highly productive agglutinative morphology
- relatively strong digital language resources
- extensive NLP research
- productive suffix chains
- rich derivational morphology
- large multilingual corpora

This makes Turkish an ideal language for evaluating multilingual AI systems across both morphological and language-family boundaries.
